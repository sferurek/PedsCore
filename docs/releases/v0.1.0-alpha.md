# PedsCore v0.1.0-alpha

## Summary

PedsCore v0.1.0-alpha is the first public alpha release of the project. It provides a bilingual, static, open-source pediatric and neonatal clinical tools library with a catalog-driven web interface, evidence traceability, dynamic forms, initial validated calculators and informational clinical rules.

Public web URL:

https://peds-core.vercel.app/

## SPRINT-50 Implementation Audit

SPRINT-50 attempted to identify safe additional implementations without
weakening the evidence gates.

- Implemented tools before SPRINT-50: 17.
- Implemented tools after SPRINT-50: 17.
- Partially implemented modules after status-model refresh: 1, WHO Growth.
- New tools implemented during SPRINT-50: none.
- Full audit document: `docs/evidence/SPRINT_50_IMPLEMENTATION_PLAN.md`.

No additional tool was promoted because all remaining candidates had at least one
blocking issue: missing complete table/formula, pending variant selection,
licensing/copyright review, maintainer decision, expert review, or high-risk
therapeutic/prognostic scope.

This decision is intentional. PedsCore prioritizes traceability and safe
implementation status over increasing the implemented count.

## WHO Growth Module

The alpha release also includes a unified WHO Growth module in
`partially_implemented` status. It is available as a documented growth workflow,
but it is not counted as a fully implemented catalog tool until final module
policy review is complete.

Current WHO Growth scope:

- WHO Child Growth Standards 0-5 core indicators:
  - BMI-for-age.
  - Weight-for-age.
  - Length/height-for-age.
  - Head circumference-for-age.
  - Weight-for-length.
  - Weight-for-height.
- WHO Growth Reference 2007 5-19 partial indicators:
  - BMI-for-age.
  - Height-for-age.
- Official WHO LMS data normalized into local static datasets.
- WHO data kept under separate WHO data licensing and attribution, not under
  the MIT source-code license.
- PedsCore-generated SVG charts, without copying official WHO chart images.
- Written percentile labels on charts: P3, P15, P50, P85 and P97.
- Visible patient point on each chart.
- Native browser print workflow.
- Guided age input:
  - date of birth plus measurement date;
  - exact age in days for 0-5;
  - structured years/months/days with approximate conversion warning;
  - completed months for 5-19.
- No storage of entered clinical data and no clinical analytics.

WHO Growth safety limitations:

- No automatic nutritional diagnosis.
- No treatment, referral or management recommendations.
- Results must be interpreted with clinical assessment, longitudinal growth
  pattern and local protocols.
- The global module remains `partially_implemented` pending final maintainer
  review, interpolation policy and remaining 5-19 scope.

## Tools In Validation

Several cataloged tools remain in validation because they require one or more of:

- Primary source confirmation.
- Complete scoring table.
- Exact variant selection.
- Published interpretation bands or cut-offs.
- Licensing or copyright review.
- Expert clinical review.

Examples include PEWS variants, Wood-Downes-Ferres, Pediatric Glasgow Coma Scale, CRIES, CHEOPS, Thompson HIE, PIPP/PIPP-R, COMFORTneo, Finnegan, remaining growth percentile scope, intensive care scores and resuscitation algorithms.

## Catalog-only And License-sensitive Tools

Some tools are intentionally catalog-only or blocked because of licensing, copyright, regulatory risk or maintainer decision requirements. These include, among others, Wong-Baker Faces, Bayley, Denver II, Orbegozo-related data review, some proprietary or license-sensitive screening tools, and high-risk official algorithms.

## Limitations

- Alpha release: content, wording and UX are still under active review.
- Not every cataloged tool has active calculation.
- Some Spanish clinical wording is not yet validated as literal Spain Spanish source wording.
- WHO Growth remains pending validation as a unified module while interpolation,
  final maintainer review and remaining 5-19 scope are decided.
- No account system or clinical data persistence exists.
- No toxicology tools are included in this phase.
- Some implemented tools still require ongoing source wording, licensing and expert review before broader clinical use.

## Disclaimer

PedsCore is an educational and informational reference for healthcare professionals. It does not replace clinical judgment, local protocols, institutional requirements, specialist consultation or individual patient assessment. It must not be used as the sole basis for clinical decision-making.

PedsCore does not store patient data.
